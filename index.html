SpectralSplats: Robust Differentiable Tracking via Spectral Moment Supervision

Avigail Cohen Rimon, Amir Mann, Mirela Ben Chen, Or Litany
Paper Supplementary Code (coming soon) Citation
SpectralSplats enables robust tracking even from zero-overlap initializations by shifting supervision from the spatial domain to spectral moments.

Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) enables real-time photorealistic rendering and is highly attractive for model-based video tracking. However, standard photometric tracking fails under severe misalignment because localized Gaussian primitives receive vanishing gradients when rendered content does not overlap the target.

We introduce SpectralSplats, a robust tracking framework that replaces local spatial supervision with global Spectral Moment supervision. By optimizing in the frequency domain, our method establishes a global basin of attraction even when spatial overlap is completely absent. We further derive a principled Frequency Annealing schedule that transitions from coarse global alignment to fine spatial precision.

Method Overview

Why pixel loss fails

Standard photometric loss vanishes under large spatial displacement, while spectral supervision provides informative gradients and avoids the locality trap.

Frequency annealing

The active frequency bandwidth expands gradually, enabling stable global alignment first and precise high-frequency refinement later.

Key Idea

Pixels are local: when the rendered object is far from the target, gradients disappear. Spectral moments are global: a spatial displacement induces a phase shift in frequency space, which produces a non-vanishing optimization signal even under zero overlap.

Low frequencies provide a wide basin of attraction, while higher frequencies are introduced progressively to recover fine geometric detail without phase-wrapping traps.

2D Demo

A simple 2D demo showing that pixel-space supervision remains stranded near initialization, while spectral supervision produces coherent motion toward the target.

Quantitative Results

Robustness under increasing initial spatial misalignment. Pixel-only supervision degrades rapidly as the initial shift grows, while SpectralSplats remains substantially more stable.

Qualitative Results

SC4D

On synthetic SC4D sequences, our method recovers pose and structure more reliably under large spatial shifts.

GART

On real-world GART data, SpectralSplats better recovers pose and preserves structure, while pixel-only optimization often becomes blurry or misaligned.
